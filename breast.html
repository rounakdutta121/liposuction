Not Sure Which Breast Procedure Is Right for You?

Many patients are confused between breast augmentation, breast reduction, and breast lift. That is completely normal. The right option depends on your concern:

Choose Augmentation if:

You want more volume, fuller shape, or better breast proportion.

Choose Reduction if:

You want smaller, lighter breasts and relief from heaviness or discomfort.

Choose Lift if:

You are happy with your breast size but want better lift, firmness, and position.

Choose Combination if:

You need both shape correction and size change (e.g., lift with implants).

* The final recommendation should always be made after consultation with a qualified plastic surgeon.

Breast Augmentation Consultation

Breast augmentation is a surgical procedure used to increase breast volume and improve breast shape.

It may be considered by women who:

  • Want fuller breasts
  • Have naturally smaller volume
  • Have volume loss post-pregnancy
  • Want better body proportion
  • Want correction of mild asymmetry
Breast Implants

Suitable for women who want a more noticeable increase in size and shape.

Breast Fat Transfer

Uses fat from your own body to create a softer and more natural enhancement. Suitable for subtle to moderate volume improvement if you have enough donor fat.

Breast Reduction Consultation

Breast reduction surgery reduces breast size and weight while reshaping the breasts. It is often chosen for both comfort and appearance.

It may be considered by women who experience:

  • Heavy breasts
  • Neck pain
  • Shoulder discomfort
  • Upper back discomfort
  • Bra strap marks
  • Difficulty finding clothing fit
  • Discomfort during physical activity
Goal: To reduce heaviness while creating a more lifted and proportionate breast shape, leading to a lighter and more balanced body shape.

Breast Lift Consultation

Breast lift surgery, also called mastopexy, helps improve breast position and shape. A breast lift does not mainly increase breast size; it focuses on lifting and reshaping.

It may be considered by women who have:

  • Sagging breasts
  • Loss of firmness
  • Downward-facing position
  • Shape changes post-pregnancy
  • Shape changes post-breastfeeding
  • Changes after weight loss
  • Age-related breast drooping

Can Procedures Be Combined?

Yes, in selected cases. Possible combinations include:

  • • Breast lift with implants
  • • Breast lift with fat transfer
  • • Breast reduction with lift
  • • As part of a wider body-contouring plan

Recovery After Surgery

Recovery depends on the procedure performed, your health, and the surgical plan. Your exact timeline will be explained during consultation.

  • Mild swelling, tightness, bruising, or discomfort initially.
  • Medicines provided for comfort.
  • Support garment or special bra recommended.
  • Light activity resumes gradually; heavy exercise restricted.
  • Final shape improves gradually as swelling settles.
